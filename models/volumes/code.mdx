---
title: "Ticket Code"
---

## Overview

Ticket codes identify the type of custody transfer or inventory movement represented by a ticket. These standardized codes help categorize and track different kinds of volume measurements and transfers throughout the production and transportation process, enabling accurate accounting, reconciliation, and reporting.

## Attributes

<ResponseField name="INV">
  Inventory - Used for tracking product inventory levels and movements within a
  facility.
</ResponseField>

<ResponseField name="BLEND">
  Product blend - Indicates mixing or blending of different product streams or
  qualities.
</ResponseField>

<ResponseField name="INJ">
  Injected product - Used when product is being injected, such as into a well or
  pipeline system.
</ResponseField>

<ResponseField name="DISP">
  Disposition - Indicates final disposition or allocation of product volumes.
</ResponseField>

<ResponseField name="LOCAL">
  Local transfer between facilities within same organization - Used for internal
  movements.
</ResponseField>

<ResponseField name="REC">
  Received product - Indicates product volumes received at a facility.
</ResponseField>

<ResponseField name="SHIP">
  Shipped product - Indicates product volumes shipped from a facility.
</ResponseField>

<ResponseField name="SHIP_INV">
  Shipped inventory - Used for tracking shipped volumes against inventory.
</ResponseField>

<ResponseField name="SALE">
  Sold inventory - Indicates product volumes that have been sold.
</ResponseField>

<ResponseField name="PRE_INV">
  Measurement before inventory point - Used for measurements taken before
  product enters inventory.
</ResponseField>

<ResponseField name="POST_INV">
  Measurement after inventory point - Used for measurements taken after product
  leaves inventory.
</ResponseField>

<ResponseField name="IN_OUT">
  General inbound/outbound movement between facilities - Used for tracking
  inter-facility transfers.
</ResponseField>
